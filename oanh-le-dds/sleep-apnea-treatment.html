Sleep Apnea Treatment

Sleep apnea treatment at Dr. Le's office in San Mateo, CA involves the use of customized oral appliances — devices that help a patient sleep safely and comfortably through the night.

What is an oral appliance?

A custom-fitted plastic or silicone device worn during sleep to help keep the airway open and prevent the breathing interruptions common in sleep apnea. Some focus on maintaining airflow, others on moving the jaw forward to improve the bite (sometimes alongside braces), and some address both airflow and snoring. Dr. Le works with each patient to determine the best fit.

Who should get an oral appliance?

People with sleep apnea who are otherwise healthy, not overweight, and without jaw malformation tend to benefit most — especially those who experience disturbances while sleeping on their backs. Appliances are generally recommended for patients under 65, though they can be used at any age. Very difficult cases may need to pair an appliance with CPAP therapy.

What's the advantage of a custom appliance over a generic one?

A custom appliance is molded to your mouth, so it rarely shifts and works at peak effectiveness all night — something generic, one-size-fits-all devices can't reliably do.

Tooth Extraction

Tooth extraction is needed in a variety of situations, both urgent and nonurgent. Dr. Le offers patients in San Mateo, CA extractions along with many other dental services.

Why are tooth extractions needed?

The most common reason today is severe periodontal disease, where the affected tooth must be removed to protect the surrounding healthy teeth. Trauma (such as a sports injury), a crowded mouth without room for adult teeth to come in, or a tooth too badly broken to restore can also require extraction.

How does the extraction work?

Dr. Le numbs the area first, with a mild sedative available for patients with dental anxiety if needed. She uses a dental tool called forceps to remove the tooth, cleans the area, and places gauze for you to bite down on to stop the bleeding. Larger extractions may need a few small stitches to keep the gums intact.

What's recovery like?

Most people need a couple of days to heal, longer for multiple or more complex extractions. Prescribed medication can help with pain, and a soft diet is recommended for at least a day afterward. Dr. Le gives each patient specific recovery instructions.
